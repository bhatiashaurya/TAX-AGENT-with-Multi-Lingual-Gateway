# Tax Risk Management Framework

## Risk categories

Compliance risk: missed filings, late deposits, defective returns — measurable through a deadline-adherence dashboard. Position risk: uncertain technical positions (classification, ITC eligibility, PE exposure, TP pricing) — track in a tax position register with likelihood and exposure quantified. Operational risk: wrong master data (PAN, GSTIN, HSN), broken interfaces between billing and returns, manual journal adjustments. Reputational and penalty risk: fraud-section notices, prosecution provisions, director liability under Section 179.

## Scoring and the risk register

A workable register records: risk description, tax type, financial year, gross exposure (tax + interest + penalty), likelihood (1-5), detection difficulty, owner, mitigation and status. Exposure times likelihood gives the ranking; anything above the materiality line goes to the audit committee quarterly. Provisioning follows accounting standards: probable outflows are provided; possible ones are disclosed as contingent liabilities.

## Leading indicators

GSTR-2B versus claimed ITC gap trending up; vendor GST non-filer count; TDS interest paid in the year (any amount signals process failure); number of open notices past first hearing; refunds pending beyond 60 days; frequency of revised returns. Each of these is cheap to compute monthly and predicts departmental attention.

## Controls that reduce risk fastest

Automated reconciliation of books to GSTR-1/3B/2B monthly; a vendor onboarding gate that verifies GSTIN status and filing history; a contract review step that fixes TDS section and rate before first payment; maker-checker on all challans; a single calendar with owner and backup per deadline; and document retention of eight years aligned to reassessment windows.

## Escalation and governance

Define thresholds: any fraud-section notice (Section 74, 148 with large escapement), any exposure above the board-set materiality, or any prosecution provision triggers CFO and board notification. Maintain a litigation tracker with counsel opinions and next hearing dates. An annual tax health check — mock audit across GST, TDS and corporate tax — catches drift before the department does.
