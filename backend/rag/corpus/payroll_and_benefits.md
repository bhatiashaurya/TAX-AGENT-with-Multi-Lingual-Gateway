# Payroll Taxation: Salary Structuring, HRA and Perquisites

## Salary TDS mechanics

Employers estimate the employee's annual tax under the chosen regime and deduct proportionately each month under Section 192. Employees declare investments at the start of the year and submit proofs by January; the employer must obtain Form 12BB. New joiners should hand over Form 12B with previous-employer income so TDS is computed on the full year. Employees choose the regime with the employer each year; the ITR choice can still differ.

## HRA exemption (old regime)

Exempt HRA is the least of: actual HRA received; rent paid minus 10% of salary (basic + DA); and 50% of salary in metro cities (40% non-metro). Rent above ₹1,00,000 per year requires the landlord's PAN; paying rent to parents is legitimate if actually paid and declared in their returns. No HRA exemption exists in the new regime.

## Perquisite valuation highlights

Rent-free accommodation: valued at 10% of salary for cities above 40 lakh population (7.5%/5% for smaller), less rent recovered. Company car: ₹1,800-₹3,300 per month depending on engine size and use mix. ESOPs: taxed as perquisite on exercise at FMV minus exercise price (startups under Section 80-IAC may defer); capital gains apply on eventual sale. Employer PF contributions above ₹7.5 lakh a year (aggregate with NPS and superannuation) are taxable, and interest on employee PF contributions above ₹2.5 lakh is taxable.

## Common structuring levers

New-regime standard deduction (₹75,000), employer NPS under 80CCD(2) up to 14% of basic (allowed even in the new regime), gratuity exemption to ₹20 lakh, leave encashment exemption at retirement to ₹25 lakh, meal cards ₹50 per meal, telephone reimbursements and LTA (old regime, twice in a four-year block). Reimbursement heads must be actual-expense based with bills; flat allowances are fully taxable.

## Employer compliance

Deposit TDS by the 7th, file 24Q quarterly with Annexure II in Q4, issue Form 16 by 15 June, and reconcile 24Q salary totals with books and PF/ESI filings — mismatches surface in employee 26AS complaints and departmental short-deduction notices.
