# Tax Audit and Departmental Audit Preparation

## Statutory tax audit (Section 44AB)

A tax audit is mandatory where business turnover exceeds ₹1 crore — relaxed to ₹10 crore where cash receipts and cash payments are each 5% or less of totals — or where professional gross receipts exceed ₹50 lakh. Presumptive taxpayers who declare profits below the deemed rate and exceed the basic exemption also need audit. The auditor files Form 3CA (audited entities) or 3CB, with particulars in Form 3CD, by 30 September. Missing the audit deadline attracts a penalty under Section 271B of 0.5% of turnover, capped at ₹1,50,000.

## Form 3CD hotspots

Clause 21 (amounts debited but disallowable), clause 26 (Section 43B items — GST, PF/ESI paid after due dates), clause 34 (TDS compliance table), clause 44 (expenditure by GST registration status). Auditors reconcile turnover with GST returns; unexplained gaps between GSTR-9 and books are the most common audit query.

## Preparing for a GST departmental audit (Section 65)

The department gives 15 working days' notice in ADT-01. Keep ready: GSTR-1/3B/9/9C with books reconciliation, ITC register mapped to GSTR-2B, e-way bill data, vendor tax-payment confirmations for major credits, HSN-rate mapping, and reconciliations of income per 26AS versus GST turnover. Findings are issued in ADT-02; discrepancies usually route into DRC-01A for tax with interest.

## Income tax scrutiny support

For a 143(3) scrutiny, maintain an evidence file per notice question: bank statements mapped to income, loan confirmations with lender PAN and creditworthiness proof (Section 68 cash credits), invoices and agreements for large expenses, valuation reports for property transactions, and digital books extracts. Consistency across ITR, financial statements, GST returns and TDS statements is what closes scrutiny quickly; contradictions are what expand it.

## Internal audit readiness controls

A monthly closing checklist — GST reconciliations, TDS deposit proofs, advance tax tracking, related-party transaction log, fixed-asset additions with ITC treatment — turns audit season into an assembly job instead of an investigation.
