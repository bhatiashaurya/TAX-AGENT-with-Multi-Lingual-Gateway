# GST Fundamentals

Goods and Services Tax (GST) is India's unified indirect tax on the supply of goods and services, replacing excise duty, service tax, VAT and several cesses. It is a destination-based consumption tax levied at each stage of the supply chain with full input tax credit (ITC), so tax sticks only on final consumption.

## Structure and rates

GST is dual: the Centre levies CGST and states levy SGST on intra-state supplies, while inter-state supplies attract IGST (collected by the Centre and apportioned). The main rate slabs are 0%, 5%, 12%, 18% and 28%, with cess on luxury and sin goods such as tobacco, aerated drinks and large cars. Essential items like unbranded food grains are exempt, and exports are zero-rated.

## Registration

GST registration is mandatory once aggregate turnover crosses ₹40 lakh for goods (₹20 lakh in special category states) or ₹20 lakh for services (₹10 lakh in special category states). Registration is also compulsory regardless of turnover for inter-state suppliers of goods, e-commerce operators and sellers, casual taxable persons, and those liable under reverse charge. Each registration is tied to a 15-character GSTIN derived from the PAN.

## Composition scheme

Small taxpayers with turnover up to ₹1.5 crore (₹75 lakh in special category states) may opt for the composition scheme: a flat 1% rate for traders and manufacturers, 5% for restaurants, and 6% for small service providers up to ₹50 lakh turnover. Composition dealers cannot collect tax from customers, cannot claim ITC, and cannot make inter-state outward supplies.

## Input tax credit

ITC lets a registered buyer offset the GST paid on inputs against output liability. To claim ITC the buyer needs a valid tax invoice, actual receipt of the goods or services, supplier payment of the tax to government, and the invoice appearing in GSTR-2B. ITC must be reversed if the supplier invoice is unpaid beyond 180 days. Blocked credits under Section 17(5) include motor vehicles for personal use, food and beverages, club memberships, and goods used for personal consumption.

## E-invoicing and e-way bills

E-invoicing is mandatory for businesses with aggregate turnover above ₹5 crore; invoices are registered on the Invoice Registration Portal (IRP) which returns a signed QR code and IRN. An e-way bill is required for movement of goods worth more than ₹50,000, generated on the e-way bill portal before transport begins.
