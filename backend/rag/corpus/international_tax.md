# International Tax and DTAA

## Residence and scope

An individual is resident in India if present 182 days or more in the year (or 60 days plus 365 over the prior four years, with concessions for citizens working abroad). Residents are taxed on worldwide income; non-residents only on income received, accruing or sourced in India. RNOR (resident but not ordinarily resident) status shields foreign-sourced income for returning NRIs for up to three years. A company is resident if incorporated in India or if its place of effective management (POEM) is in India.

## Double Taxation Avoidance Agreements

India has DTAAs with over 90 countries. Treaty relief typically caps withholding on dividends (5-15%), interest (10-15%), royalties and fees for technical services (10-15%), and allocates business profits to India only where a permanent establishment (PE) exists. To claim treaty benefits a non-resident needs a Tax Residency Certificate from the home jurisdiction, Form 10F filed electronically, and a no-PE declaration. Section 90(2) lets the taxpayer apply whichever is more beneficial, treaty or domestic law, subject to GAAR and the treaty's Principal Purpose Test under the MLI.

## Payments abroad and Form 15CA/15CB

Any remittance to a non-resident that is chargeable to tax requires TDS under Section 195 at rates-in-force or the treaty rate if lower. The remitter files Form 15CA online, with a CA certificate in Form 15CB where the remittance exceeds ₹5 lakh and is taxable. Grossing-up applies where the Indian payer bears the tax.

## Foreign assets and reporting

Resident taxpayers must disclose foreign bank accounts, equity, immovable property and beneficial interests in Schedule FA of the ITR; failures attract Black Money Act penalties of ₹10 lakh per year and potential prosecution. Foreign tax credit is claimed in Form 67, filed before the return, supported by proof of foreign tax paid.

## Equalisation levy and significant economic presence

The 6% equalisation levy applies to online advertising payments to non-residents; the broader 2% e-commerce levy was withdrawn from August 2024. Significant Economic Presence rules can create Indian nexus for non-residents with large digital user bases even without physical presence, subject to treaty override.
