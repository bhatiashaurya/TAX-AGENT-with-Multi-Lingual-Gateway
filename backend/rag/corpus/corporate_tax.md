# Corporate Tax in India

## Rates for domestic companies

The default corporate rate is 30% (25% where turnover in the reference year is up to ₹400 crore). Most companies instead elect Section 115BAA: a 22% rate (effective 25.17% with 10% surcharge and 4% cess) in exchange for giving up most exemptions and incentives, with no Minimum Alternate Tax. New domestic manufacturing companies incorporated after 1 October 2019 that commence manufacturing by the sunset date may elect Section 115BAB at 15% (effective 17.16%). Once exercised, these options are irrevocable.

## MAT and book profits

Companies outside 115BAA/115BAB pay Minimum Alternate Tax at 15% of book profit where normal tax is lower. MAT credit can be carried forward 15 years and set off when normal tax exceeds MAT. Book profit starts from the P&L prepared under the Companies Act with prescribed additions (income tax, dividend adjustments) and deductions (brought-forward loss or unabsorbed depreciation, whichever is lower).

## Dividends and buybacks

Dividends are taxable in shareholders' hands at slab rates, with companies deducting TDS at 10% under Section 194 above ₹5,000. From October 2024, buyback proceeds are treated as dividend income for shareholders.

## Losses and depreciation

Business losses carry forward eight years and set off only against business income; unabsorbed depreciation carries forward indefinitely and sets off against any income. Carry-forward requires filing the return by the due date. In closely held companies, a 51% continuity of beneficial shareholding is required to carry forward losses (Section 79), with startup relaxations.

## Compliance calendar for companies

Advance tax follows the 15/45/75/100 schedule in June, September, December and March. The tax audit report under Section 44AB (Form 3CA/3CB with 3CD) is due 30 September where turnover exceeds ₹1 crore (₹10 crore where cash transactions are under 5%). The return (ITR-6) is due 31 October, or 30 November with transfer pricing. Form 3CEB for international/specified domestic transactions is due 31 October.
