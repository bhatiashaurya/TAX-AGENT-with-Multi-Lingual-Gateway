# ITR Filing: Forms, Deadlines and Refunds

## Which ITR form

ITR-1 (Sahaj) suits resident individuals with income up to ₹50 lakh from salary, one house property and other sources. ITR-2 covers individuals and HUFs with capital gains, more than one house property, foreign assets or income above ₹50 lakh. ITR-3 is for individuals with business or professional income. ITR-4 (Sugam) covers presumptive taxation under Sections 44AD (business, 8%/6% of turnover), 44ADA (professionals, 50% of gross receipts up to ₹75 lakh) and 44AE. Companies file ITR-6 and trusts file ITR-7.

## Deadlines and consequences

The due date for individuals not requiring audit is 31 July following the financial year; audited taxpayers get until 31 October, and transfer-pricing cases until 30 November. A belated or revised return may be filed until 31 December of the assessment year. Late filing attracts a fee under Section 234F of ₹5,000 (₹1,000 where total income is below ₹5 lakh), interest under 234A, loss of the ability to carry forward most losses, and possible delayed refunds. The updated return (ITR-U) allows correcting omissions up to four years after the assessment year with additional tax of up to 70%.

## E-verification

An ITR is not treated as filed until verified within 30 days: options include Aadhaar OTP, net banking, bank-account EVC, or posting the signed ITR-V to CPC Bengaluru. Unverified returns are treated as never filed.

## Income tax refunds

Refunds arise when TDS, TCS or advance tax exceed the final liability. After processing under Section 143(1) at CPC, refunds are credited directly to the pre-validated bank account linked to PAN. Track status on the e-filing portal under Services, then Know Your Refund Status, or on the TIN-NSDL refund tracker. Interest under Section 244A accrues at 0.5% per month on most refunds. Common blockers: bank account not pre-validated, PAN-Aadhaar not linked (return treated as defective), outstanding demand adjustment under Section 245 (an intimation is issued first), or a mismatch between Form 26AS/AIS and the return.
