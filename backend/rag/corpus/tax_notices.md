# Income Tax and GST Notices: Reading and Responding

## Common income tax notices

Section 143(1) intimation is an automated adjustment summary after processing — compare column A (as filed) with column B (as computed); a demand or refund follows. Respond within 30 days if you disagree, via a rectification (Section 154) or by agreeing and paying. Section 139(9) marks a defective return (missing schedules, unlinked PAN-Aadhaar, unpaid self-assessment tax) — cure it within 15 days or the return is treated as invalid. Section 142(1) seeks documents or a return where none was filed. Section 143(2) begins scrutiny assessment; it must be issued within three months of the end of the financial year in which the return was filed. Section 148/148A reopens past assessments where income escaped assessment — 148A requires a show-cause opportunity first; reopening runs 3 years normally, extended to 5 years (post-2024 rules) where escaped income is ₹50 lakh or more. Section 245 intimates adjustment of a refund against past demand — respond within 21 days to contest.

## Responding well

Every notice carries a DIN (Document Identification Number) — verify it on the e-filing portal; a notice without a valid DIN is invalid. Respond only through the e-proceedings tab, within the stated time, attaching indexed evidence. Ask for adjournment before the deadline if needed. Never ignore a notice: best-judgment assessment under Section 144, penalties under 270A (50% of tax on under-reporting, 200% for misreporting) and prosecution exposure follow non-response.

## Common GST notices

ASMT-10 flags return discrepancies (GSTR-1 vs 3B, ITC vs 2B) — reply in ASMT-11 within 30 days. DRC-01A is a pre-show-cause intimation allowing payment with reduced penalty; DRC-01 is the formal show-cause under Section 73 (non-fraud: penalty 10% of tax) or Section 74 (fraud: penalty up to 100%). REG-17 threatens registration cancellation; reply in REG-18 within 7 days. Appeals against orders go to the Appellate Authority within 3 months with a 10% pre-deposit of disputed tax.

## Practical checklist on receiving any notice

Verify DIN and PAN/GSTIN; diarise the deadline; reconcile the figures the department relied on (26AS, AIS, GSTR-2B); draft a point-wise reply with annexures; keep proof of submission. Escalate to a professional where the demand is large, fraud sections are invoked, or reassessment is proposed.
