# Income Tax for Individuals

## New regime slabs (FY 2024-25, AY 2025-26)

The new (default) regime taxes total income as follows: nil up to ₹3,00,000; 5% from ₹3,00,001 to ₹7,00,000; 10% from ₹7,00,001 to ₹10,00,000; 15% from ₹10,00,001 to ₹12,00,000; 20% from ₹12,00,001 to ₹15,00,000; and 30% above ₹15,00,000. A standard deduction of ₹50,000 applies to salary income (₹75,000 from FY 2024-25 for the new regime). The Section 87A rebate makes tax zero where taxable income does not exceed ₹7,00,000. Health and education cess of 4% applies on the tax amount, and surcharge applies to incomes above ₹50 lakh (capped at 25% under the new regime).

## Old regime and choosing between regimes

The old regime keeps the ₹2,50,000 basic exemption with 5%, 20% and 30% slabs but allows deductions: Section 80C up to ₹1,50,000 (PF, PPF, ELSS, LIC, principal on home loan), Section 80D medical insurance (₹25,000, or ₹50,000 for senior citizens), HRA exemption, home-loan interest under Section 24(b) up to ₹2,00,000, and NPS additional ₹50,000 under 80CCD(1B). The new regime is generally better below roughly ₹3.75 lakh of total deductions; salaried taxpayers can switch regimes every year, while business income earners can switch back only once.

## Capital gains (post-July 2024 rules)

Listed equity and equity mutual funds held over 12 months are long-term: LTCG is taxed at 12.5% on gains above ₹1.25 lakh per year; short-term gains are taxed at 20%. Other assets (property, gold, unlisted shares) held over 24 months are long-term and taxed at 12.5% without indexation. Property sellers may claim exemption by reinvesting under Section 54 (residential house) or 54EC bonds up to ₹50 lakh.

## Advance tax and self-assessment

Advance tax applies when the year's tax liability exceeds ₹10,000: 15% by 15 June, 45% by 15 September, 75% by 15 December and 100% by 15 March. Shortfalls attract interest under Sections 234B and 234C at 1% per month. Salaried taxpayers usually meet this through TDS, but capital gains and interest income often require top-up payments.
