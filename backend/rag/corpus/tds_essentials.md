# TDS and TCS Essentials

## Common TDS sections and rates

Salary TDS under Section 192 follows the employee's slab. Key non-salary rates: 194A interest other than securities 10% (threshold ₹40,000, ₹50,000 for senior citizens); 194C contractor payments 1% for individuals/HUF and 2% for others (threshold ₹30,000 single payment or ₹1,00,000 annual); 194H commission 5%; 194I rent 10% for land/building and 2% for plant/machinery (threshold ₹2,40,000 per year); 194J professional fees 10% and technical services 2% (threshold ₹30,000); 194Q purchase of goods 0.1% above ₹50 lakh. Section 194IA requires the buyer of property above ₹50 lakh to deduct 1% of the sale value. Without PAN, TDS applies at 20% under Section 206AA.

## Deposit and return deadlines

TDS deducted in a month must be deposited by the 7th of the next month (30 April for March deductions). Quarterly TDS returns are due: Q1 by 31 July, Q2 by 31 October, Q3 by 31 January and Q4 by 31 May — Form 24Q for salary, 26Q for resident non-salary, 27Q for payments to non-residents. TDS certificates follow: Form 16 (salary, annual, by 15 June) and Form 16A (quarterly, within 15 days of the return due date).

## Interest, fees and penalties

Late deduction attracts interest at 1% per month; deduction made but late deposit attracts 1.5% per month from the date of deduction. Late filing of TDS returns costs ₹200 per day under Section 234E (capped at the TDS amount), and Section 271H penalties range ₹10,000 to ₹1,00,000 for continued default or incorrect statements. Disallowance under Section 40(a)(ia) hits 30% of the expense where TDS was not deducted or deposited.

## TDS corrections and mismatches

Deductees should reconcile Form 26AS and AIS with their own records each quarter. Mismatches usually trace to wrong PAN in the deductor's return, wrong assessment year on the challan, or unfiled deductor returns. Corrections are made by the deductor through a correction statement on TRACES; challan errors (minor head, AY) can be fixed via OLTAS challan correction. A TDS correction request generally reflects in Form 26AS within 7 to 10 days of processing.

## TCS

Tax Collected at Source applies to specified transactions: sale of scrap 1%, motor vehicles above ₹10 lakh 1%, and foreign remittances under LRS 20% above ₹7 lakh (5% for education/medical financed by loan). TCS returns are filed quarterly in Form 27EQ with certificates in Form 27D.
