# GST Returns, Payments and Refunds

## Return filing

GSTR-1 reports outward supplies and is due by the 11th of the following month (quarterly under QRMP for turnover up to ₹5 crore, due the 13th after the quarter). GSTR-3B is the monthly summary return with tax payment, due the 20th of the following month (22nd/24th for QRMP taxpayers by state). GSTR-9 is the annual return due 31 December following the financial year, and GSTR-9C is the self-certified reconciliation statement mandatory above ₹5 crore turnover. Composition dealers file CMP-08 quarterly and GSTR-4 annually.

## Late fees and interest

Late filing of GSTR-3B attracts a late fee of ₹50 per day (₹20 for nil returns), capped based on turnover, plus interest at 18% per annum on unpaid tax. Interest on wrongly availed and utilised ITC is 24% per annum. Consistent non-filing for six months can lead to registration cancellation.

## GST refunds

Refunds arise from exports (with or without payment of tax under LUT), inverted duty structure (input rate higher than output rate), excess balance in the electronic cash ledger, and finalisation of provisional assessments. A refund application is filed in Form RFD-01 within two years of the relevant date. The officer must issue an acknowledgement in RFD-02 within 15 days and sanction the refund within 60 days of a complete application; delays beyond 60 days attract 6% interest payable to the taxpayer.

For export refunds, 90% can be sanctioned provisionally within 7 days of acknowledgement. Refund status can be tracked on the GST portal under Services, then Refunds, then Track Application Status using the ARN. Common rejection reasons include mismatch between GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B, missing shipping bill details, ITC claimed on blocked credits, and deficiency memos (RFD-03) not answered within 15 days.

## Reconciliation discipline

Monthly reconciliation between books, GSTR-1, GSTR-3B and GSTR-2B is the single most effective compliance control. Differences between GSTR-2B and claimed ITC are the leading trigger for departmental notices in Form ASMT-10 and DRC-01. Maintain a vendor-wise ITC ageing so credits blocked by non-filing vendors are chased before the September cut-off following the financial year, after which unclaimed ITC lapses.
