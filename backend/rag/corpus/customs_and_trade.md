# Customs Duties and Trade Compliance

## Duty structure on imports

Import duty stacks in this order: Basic Customs Duty (BCD) on assessable value; Social Welfare Surcharge at 10% of BCD; IGST on (value + BCD + SWS) at the GST rate of the goods; and, where applicable, Agriculture Infrastructure and Development Cess (AIDC), anti-dumping duty, or safeguard duty. IGST paid on imports is available as input tax credit; BCD is a cost.

## Valuation

Customs value follows the transaction value under the Customs Valuation Rules — the price actually paid plus additions such as royalties tied to the imported goods, freight and insurance (CIF basis). Related-party imports go through Special Valuation Branch (SVB) scrutiny to confirm the relationship did not influence price.

## Classification and exemptions

Goods are classified under 8-digit HSN codes in the Customs Tariff; classification drives BCD rate, IGST rate and licensing requirements. Exemptions and concessional rates flow from notifications and Free Trade Agreements — claiming an FTA rate requires a valid Certificate of Origin and, under CAROTAR, importers must possess origin information beyond the certificate.

## Key procedures

Imports clear through the Bill of Entry filed on ICEGATE, ideally in advance; late filing attracts charges. Exports move on a Shipping Bill, which doubles as the refund claim for zero-rated GST exports. AEO (Authorised Economic Operator) status grants faster clearances and deferred duty payment. IEC (Import Export Code) from DGFT is a prerequisite for any import/export.

## Duty savings and disputes

Duty drawback refunds duties on inputs used in exports; Advance Authorisation allows duty-free import of inputs for export production; EPCG permits capital-goods import at zero BCD against export obligations. Disputes start with a show-cause notice; remedies run through adjudication, Commissioner (Appeals), and CESTAT. Voluntary payment with interest before notice caps penalties under Section 28.
